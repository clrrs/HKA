Women's Rights Story 1: The Wings of Change

The Woman's Suffrage Movement

Throughout her life, Helen Keller defied categorization, actively championing both Disability Rights and Women's Suffrage. She was deeply involved in the Suffragist movement, delivering speeches, engaging with political leaders, and attending rallies. Despite facing criticism and attempts to undermine her autonomy by suggesting her ideas were not her own, Keller's commitment to advocating for women's rights never wavered. Her persistence and fearless advocacy were encapsulated in her 1920 speech, "Why Woman Wants to Vote," where she confidently stated, “Today, women are asserting their rights; tomorrow, nobody will be foolhardy enough to question them.”
