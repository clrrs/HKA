Civil Rights: You Can't Kill Ideas

Letter to Adolf Hitler / German students, 1933


Letter Transcript
Letter Guided Description

Justice is Immortal

As totalitarianism took hold of Germany in the nineteen thirties, the Nazi regime's book burnings symbolized a brutal repression of culture. Helen Keller responded with conviction, asserting that despite being burned, the ideas within books would survive and continue to inspire others. "You can burn my books and the books of the best minds in Europe, but the ideas in them have seeped through a million channels, and will continue to quicken other minds." Her letter was a testament to the immortality of ideas, standing as a beacon of hope and resistance against tyranny.
