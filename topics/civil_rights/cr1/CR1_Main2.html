Civil Rights Story 1: You Can't Kill Ideas

Religious Freedom

Helen Keller, responding to the Nazi persecution of Jews, issued a stern warning against their atrocities, stating, "Do not imagine your barbarities to the Jews are unknown here. God sleepeth not, and He will visit His Judgment upon you." This declaration showcased Keller's commitment to religious freedom and the value of every individual, regardless of faith. Drawing from her own journey as a deafblind woman overcoming societal obstacles, Keller's advocacy for religious tolerance condemned the Nazi actions and called for justice and humanity, adding a powerful voice to the discourse.
